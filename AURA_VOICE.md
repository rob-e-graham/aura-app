## Five principles

### 1. Calm, not excited
AURA is the friend who texts back on a Tuesday afternoon, not the one
who DMs in caps. There's no FOMO, no urgency, no "transform your life
in 5 days". Excitement leaks energy. Calm holds it.

> ❌ "Pull a card! Discover your destiny!! ✨🔮✨"
>
> ✅ "Pull a card. Sit with the colour. See what comes up."

### 2. Curious, not authoritative
Cards don't tell people who they are. They give them something to think
about. AURA always invites — never declares.

> ❌ "Your future is calling. Stop ignoring the signs."
>
> ✅ "What does this card make you notice today?"

### 3. Beautiful, not busy
Short sentences. Generous whitespace. One idea per beat. If a sentence
feels heavy, cut it in half. If a paragraph has more than three
sentences, ask if it needs them all.

> ❌ "AURA is a revolutionary new app that combines AI technology with traditional tarot wisdom to deliver personalised, transformative readings tailored to your unique journey of self-discovery."
>
> ✅ "AURA is a card reading app. Calmer than the others."

### 4. Honest about what it is
AURA isn't fortune telling. It isn't therapy. It isn't psychic. It's
a beautifully designed reflection space, with AI that listens better
than most. Say what it is. Skip the mystical theatre.

> ❌ "Channel ancient cosmic wisdom."
>
> ✅ "A daily ritual for self-discovery."

### 5. Lowercase often (with two exceptions)
Lowercase reads softer. AURA the brand stays in its uppercase
all-caps treatment, but body copy frequently leans lowercase —
especially on social, where it signals warmth and informality.

> ❌ "Your Daily Reading Awaits"
>
> ✅ "today's card is ready when you are"

**Two always-Title-Case exceptions:** "Timeless Wisdom" and "Self Reflection"
— the brand pillars from the locked tagline. Even on social, capitalise
them. They're proper-noun *concepts*, not casual descriptors.

## Words AURA uses

**Reach for these:**
reflection, ritual, reading, card, orb, palette, presence,
notice, sit with, allow, gentle, quiet, soft, calm, threshold,
return, bloom, hold, pause, settle, attend, witness, hush,
unhurried, considered, true.

**Use sparingly:**
discovery, journey, energy, intuition, growth.
*(Fine in moderation, but they're the wellness clichés — earn them.)*

**Avoid completely:**
manifest, manifesting, manifestation,
fate, destiny, fortune, predict, prediction,
psychic, supernatural, magic, spell,
ancient wisdom, secret knowledge,
unlock your potential, transform your life, level up,
high vibe, low vibe, raise your frequency,
healing energy, sacred feminine.

These get the app rejected by Apple's reviewers (medical/spiritual claims)
and signal to thoughtful readers that we're a generic wellness brand.
We're not.

## Punctuation & formatting rules

**Em dashes:** yes — they hold a beat the way commas can't. Use them.
**Exclamation marks:** rare. Maybe one per email, never two in a row,
never on social.
**Emojis:** the AURA sparkle (✦) only. No 🔮 ✨ 💫 — they're someone else's
brand. The four-pointed sparkle is ours.
**Ellipses:** very sparingly. They're the punctuation of trailing off,
which AURA doesn't do.
**Quotes:** smart quotes ("") not straight ("").
**The brand wordmark:** AURA stays uppercase. The app, the company, the
mark — all uppercase. Body copy elsewhere is lowercase-friendly.

## Forbidden moves

- **No urgency tactics.** No "limited time offers", no "last chance",
  no countdown timers.
- **No personality tests masquerading as readings.** "Are you a Bloom or
  a Threshold?" is a Buzzfeed move, not an AURA move.
- **No promising outcomes.** "Find love this week" / "Discover your
  purpose". Cards don't promise. Neither do we.
- **No targeting vulnerable moments unsubtly.** "Going through a tough
  breakup?" → no. Hold the calm; let people find the app when they're
  ready.
- **No engagement bait.** "Tag a friend who needs this." → no. The right
  reader shares unprompted.
- **No emoji storms.** One sparkle per post, max.
- **No competitor sniping.** No "Tarot apps are stuck in 1995." Be
  better quietly.

## Reference voices to study (for vibe, not for copying)

- **Aesop** — calm, considered, never sells; product copy as essay.
- **Susan Miller (Astrology Zone)** — warm, practical, gentle, never
  predictive in a "you will" way.
- **The School of Life** — clean prose, uses lowercase comfortably,
  treats readers as intelligent adults.
- **Loewe (perfumes)** — sensory, restrained, unafraid of empty space.
- **Late-period Joan Didion sentences** — short, direct, declaratives
  with an undertow.

Don't be these. Be inspired by them.

## Quick checklist before posting / sending

Before you publish anything in AURA's voice:

- [ ] Did I say what's true, not what's exciting?
- [ ] Does it sound like a calm friend, not a salesperson?
- [ ] Have I removed all unnecessary words?
- [ ] One sparkle ✦ at most?
- [ ] Zero forbidden words?
- [ ] If this were a quiet Tuesday afternoon, would this still feel right?

If yes to all → ship.
If no to any → cut, soften, simplify.
